Statistical Analysis of News Articles

About the project

Social media is a real world phenomenon of the saying “If the tree falls on the ground and no one is around to hear it, does it make a sound?” Mashable, an independent news platform, is a part of this social media world providing the latest news articles on digital culture, social media and technology. However, not every new article is a hit, and our group aims to find what makes an article popular by analyzing various factors related to the article.

Why did we work on this project?

As news and media shifts online, it provides us with an opportunity to ask and answer questions about readers and publishers, thanks to the data trail it leaves behind. This analysis can shed some light on how individuals consume online news, what are the various factors that affect their engagement with it and how have news publishers like NYT and Mashable have evolved over time to keep their audience engaged.
